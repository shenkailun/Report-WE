Research

existing solution review

There currently aren’t any wildly available solutions to the problem at hand. One example is the ‘Go Pro Surgery Package’ [1], which offer cameras which can be worn by surgeons which are able to capture high quality footage from the surgeon’s point of view, at varied levels of magnification. Whilst the footage is of high quality, from a reliable brand, that’s all they provide. The surgeons themselves would then have to worry about storing and uploading video, potentially having to pay for even more services, with an already tight budget.

existing solution review

‘Inition’ are a company who have also attempted to provide another solution, using 3D displays and motion capture technology to try and improve the quality of surgery as well as the quality of training. However, whilst the results were promising, this only provided live 3D footage to those at the screening watching with 3D glasses [2]. One interesting aspect of the research was the tracking of surgeons’ hands for future analysis, for future training purposes. One example of a related technology is the surgical video database provided by MedlinePlus [3]. The webpage provides links to a number of surgical videos. The format is clear and concise; however, links are all the page provides. There are no sort functions, video previews or the ability to search and filter videos. This makes it quite difficult to easily find desired videos.

Final decisions

After reviewing existing and related technologies, we tried to take what was best from each and implement similar techniques and functionalities into our own solution. For example, taking note of the clear format of the MedlinePlus page, or the techniques used by Inition to help improve the quality of training.
